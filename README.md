# Ludodex

**A daily word puzzle for gamers.**

Swipe to connect letters and find hidden words across a 4×4 grid. Every puzzle is built around a theme drawn from gaming history — characters, items, locations, hardware, studios, and more. One new puzzle drops every day.

---

## How to Play

### The Grid

Each puzzle gives you a 4×4 grid of 16 letters. Hidden inside are several words that all share a common theme. Your job is to find them all.

### Swiping

Drag your finger (or mouse) across **adjacent letters** to spell a word. Letters can connect horizontally, vertically, and diagonally — as long as each tile is next to the previous one. Lift when you're done.

- You can backtrack through your current chain by swiping back over the previous tile.
- Tapping a tile you've already selected removes it from the chain.
- Starting a new swipe from a non-adjacent tile begins a fresh chain.

### Words and Themes

Every puzzle has a declared theme. The answers might be:

- **Characters** — SONIC, LARA CROFT, MARIO
- **Locations** — areas, zones, or worlds from a game
- **Items** — weapons, power-ups, key objects
- **Hardware** — consoles, handhelds, accessories
- **Studios** — developers and publishers from gaming history
- **Titles** — game names, series, franchises

Some answers are multi-word (like LARA CROFT). The letters for each part appear as separate slot groups below the grid, divided by a dot.

### Hint Slots

Below the grid you'll see a row of blank slots — one per letter of each answer. These show you the shape of each word before you solve it. Once you find a word, its slots light up.

**If you're stuck**, press and hold any empty slot to reveal that letter. This spends one hint from your balance. You start with 5 hints and earn 1 more each day (up to a cap of 7 free hints). Purchased hint packs don't expire and accumulate on top.

## Scoring

When you finish a puzzle, you earn 1 to 3 stars based on how cleanly you played:

| Stars | Condition |
|-------|-----------|
| ★☆☆ | Solved with mistakes or hints |
| ★★☆ | Solved without mistakes, but used hints |
| ★★★ | **Pristine** — solved with zero mistakes and zero hints |

**Mistakes** are counted when you start a swipe chain that doesn't lead anywhere useful — wrong letter paths, abandoned chains, and misdirected guesses.

A **Pristine** solve triggers the full celebration: confetti, a glowing backdrop, and the "FLAWLESS" banner.

## Daily Puzzle & Streak

A new puzzle releases every day at **local midnight** — the same model used by Wordle, NYT Connections, and Strands. Solve the daily puzzle to keep your streak alive.

Miss a day and your streak resets. If that happens, a banner on the menu reminds you what streak you lost and invites you to start fresh.

Your best time across all solves is tracked separately from your streak.

---

## The Archive

Every past puzzle is preserved in the Archive. Tap any entry to play it and earn a star rating — archive solves don't affect your daily streak, but they do count toward your solve total and achievement progress.

On the web version, the archive shows the most recent 7 days freely. The full archive back to launch is available in the app.

## Achievements

Ludodex has 40+ achievements across six categories. They unlock automatically — no manual claiming needed.

**Streak** — keep your daily chain going. Milestones at 1, 3, 7, 14, 30, 60, 100, 200, and 365 days.

**Solves** — total puzzles completed. Milestones from your first solve up to 1,000.

**Pristine** — no-mistake, no-hint clears. From your first flawless solve up to 250, plus a secret achievement for going pristine in under 10 seconds.

**Speed** — solve time records. Achievements for finishing under 60s, 30s, 20s, 15s, and 10s.

**Consistency** — consecutive pristine solves. Hat Trick (3 in a row), Unstoppable (5), Untouchable (10).

**Variety** — everything else. Night Owl (solved between 22:00–03:00), Early Bird (05:00–08:00), Archive Diver, Archive Master, and Time Capsule (solving a puzzle that's 30+ days old).

Achievements earned before the system was introduced are retroactively unlocked on your next launch based on your existing stats.

Three visual themes ship with the game:

**Synthwave** — deep purples, magenta tiles, neon pink swipe trail. Feels like a 1980s arcade at 2 AM.

## Puzzle Design Notes

Puzzles are authored on a coordinate grid where each tile is addressed by column (a–d) and row (1–4). Every letter on the grid belongs to at least one answer path; tiles shared between answers only deactivate once all answers that claim them have been solved. Filler letters can be added to reach the full 16-tile count.

Each puzzle has a declared difficulty (easy / medium / hard) and category. Some puzzles belong to a series with multiple installments across different days.

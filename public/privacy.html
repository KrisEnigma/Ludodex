Ludodex ("the app", "we", "us") is a daily word puzzle game built by KrisEnigma. This policy explains what data the app handles, where it goes, and what choices you have.

We keep this short on purpose. The app is built to need very little about you.

Who we are

Ludodex is operated by KrisEnigma, an independent developer based in Santiago, Chile. You can reach us at kris@krisenigma.com for any privacy question.

What we store on your device

Most of what the app remembers is stored locally on your phone or browser, and never leaves your device unless you choose to share it. That includes:

  • Which puzzles you've solved and how fast
  • Your current streak and best streak
  • Your star ratings per puzzle and pristine count
  • How many hints you've used and which puzzles you've revealed letters in
  • Which achievements you've unlocked and when
  • Your language preference (English or Spanish)
  • Your selected skin
  • Whether you've seen the tutorial

This data is held by your device's secure key-value storage (Apple Preferences on iOS, SharedPreferences on Android, localStorage on web). It is not transmitted to us or to anyone else. You can wipe all of it at any time by long-pressing the version number in Settings and confirming "Reset progress", or by uninstalling the app.

What we send over the network

The app talks to a small number of third-party services. Each has its own privacy policy linked below.

Puzzle data hosting (all platforms). The app fetches its daily puzzle list from ludodex.krisenigma.com, hosted on Cloudflare Pages. Cloudflare receives standard request metadata (your IP address, your device's User-Agent string, the time of the request). We do not send any account info, progress, or identifiers with this request.

Advertising — AdMob (iOS and Android only). If you have not purchased "Remove Ads", the app shows an interstitial ad from Google AdMob every two solved puzzles. AdMob collects information including your device's advertising identifier, IP address, ad interactions, and approximate location to deliver and measure ads. On first launch in regions covered by GDPR or similar laws, the app shows Google's User Messaging Platform consent prompt so you can choose between personalised and non-personalised ads. On iOS, the app also requests App Tracking Transparency permission as required by Apple. You can disable ads entirely by purchasing "Remove Ads" inside the app, or by enabling "Limit Ad Tracking" (iOS) or "Opt out of Ads Personalization" (Android) in your device settings. AdMob's privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy.

In-app purchases — RevenueCat (iOS and Android only). Purchases are processed by Apple App Store or Google Play. RevenueCat sits between us and the stores to verify entitlements (whether you own "Remove Ads", a skin, or the bundle). RevenueCat generates an anonymous user ID tied to your device and stores your purchase history. We do not send your name, email, or any personal info to RevenueCat — only the anonymous ID and your purchase events. RevenueCat's privacy policy: https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy.

Crash reporting — Sentry (all platforms). When the app crashes or hits an unexpected error, we collect a stack trace, your app version, your device model and OS version, and the recent in-app actions ("breadcrumbs") that led up to the error. We do not include the content of your puzzle attempts, your progress data, or any identifier that ties the report back to you specifically. Reports are retained for 30 days and used solely to fix bugs. Sentry's privacy policy: https://sentry.io/privacy/.

Product analytics — PostHog (all platforms). To understand how players move through the app and what improvements actually matter, we send anonymous usage events to PostHog. The events cover: which views you open, when you start and complete puzzles, when you preview or purchase a skin, when you unlock an achievement, when you skip or complete the tutorial. Each event is tied to a randomly generated anonymous identifier stored on your device — never your name, email, or anything personally identifying. We do not use PostHog for advertising or share any data with third parties. PostHog's privacy policy: https://posthog.com/privacy.

Achievements (iOS and Android only). When you unlock an achievement, the app reports it to Apple's Game Center (iOS) or Google Play Games (Android) so it appears in your platform-level gaming profile. The reported data is the achievement identifier only. Apple's and Google's gaming privacy practices apply: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/en-ww/ and https://policies.google.com/privacy.

Notifications (iOS and Android only). The app schedules a local daily reminder using your device's notification system. The reminder is generated and shown by your device — no notification content leaves your device.

That's the full list of network calls. There are no analytics SDKs, no advertising trackers beyond AdMob, no fingerprinting, no email lists.

What we never collect

To be explicit: Ludodex does not ask for or store your name, your email address, your phone number, your contacts, your photos, your microphone, your camera, your precise location, your health data, your contacts, your browsing history outside the app, or any account login. There are no user accounts at all.

Children

The app is rated 4+ on iOS and "Everyone" on Google Play. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). On iOS, if your device is part of a Family Sharing group with Ask to Buy enabled, in-app purchases are gated through a parent or guardian. If you believe a child has used the app under conditions that would require parental consent, contact us and we will delete any associated data on request — though as described above, that data is normally only present on the child's own device.

Your rights

Because Ludodex does not maintain user accounts or store personal data on our servers, most data-access requests are satisfied by you, on your device:

  • Access: every piece of data the app stores about your play is visible inside the app itself (Settings, achievements view, archive).
  • Erase: long-press the version number in Settings → "Reset progress", or uninstall the app. Both actions are immediate and irreversible.
  • Restrict ads / purchase tracking: use your device's privacy settings to limit ad personalisation. Purchase "Remove Ads" to eliminate AdMob requests entirely.

If you are in the EU/UK (GDPR), California (CCPA/CPRA), Brazil (LGPD), or Chile (Ley 19.628) and want to make a formal request about data held by our third-party processors (AdMob, RevenueCat, Sentry, PostHog), contact us at the address below and we will forward your request to the relevant processor.

Data retention

Local device data persists until you reset it or uninstall. Third-party processors retain data according to their own policies (linked above) — typically: AdMob up to 14 months for analytics, RevenueCat for the lifetime of the anonymous ID, Sentry for 30 days.

International data transfers

The third-party services listed above are operated by U.S. companies and may transfer data outside your country of residence. Each processor has its own safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses for EU transfers, etc.) documented in their privacy policies.

Changes to this policy

If we change what data the app handles, we update this page and bump the "Last updated" date. Material changes will also be noted in the app's "What's new" text on the next release.
